About Me

Hi, I'm Arwin Silpakar, a student at Sydney Technical High School with a major passion for robotics, electronics, and all things computer-related. I love building projects from scratch, whether it’s designing a custom PCB, creating a Discord bot, or designing a custom keyboard. I enjoy learning new technologies and applying them to real-world projects.

Ever since I was young, I was fascinated by computers and how they worked. I used to see them as magical black boxes that could take inputs and produce exactly what I wanted. As I grew older, my curiosity led me to explore programming, electronics, and robotics. Over the years, I’ve become a jack of all trades, experimenting with different things to develop both my technical skills and my problem-solving abilities.

I’m a part of the UNSW Robotics team, EMU 4729 and my role is mainly coding. Using the knowledge I had gained from EMU, I started my own robotics team at school where I led a group of students to create a robot for FTC. In 2025, both the teams I was a part of managed to make it to nationals at Macquarie University where we were up against teams from all across Australia. In 2026, I have had the honour of being invited to New York to compete at a FRC robotics competition with EMU.

Academically, I’m good at all the technical subjects. Outside of computers, I love math and science. Learning about the world and how it is governed by these fundamental laws just seems so cool to me. I’ve also won many awards in relation to math, science and computer technology including duxxing Computer Technology and getting a Science Scholarship.

Looking ahead, I hope to continue creating, combining my passions in robotics, software development and computers. Every project I undertake teaches me something new and fuels my excitement to innovate further.
